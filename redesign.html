What the 2018 bullrun can tell us about Dogecoin's price



There is no doubt that Dogecoin is having quite a moment righ now. With billionares hoards of internet fans, and even people who have barely heard of crypto taking an intrest in Doge. But it makes sense, crypto is in a bull market right now, Dogecoin is a funny meme coin, and most importanly: It's on nearly every exchange, and its price per coin is miniscule compared to its more sucessful predeseors (BTC and ETH).


But Dogecoin as an investment has often been scoffed at as too risky, with fears of high price volitility, unrealistic market cap predictions, and infinate inflation. But those aside, how can the previous (2017/2018) bull run help us decide whether dog truly is moon bound, of it its just simply a popular pump and dump that will fade back into obscurity with time?



There are a couple obvious things about the last 6 months of Doge's price. First, you have mulitple instances of 400% price increases, with the first happening in late Janurary, and the second in early April. These increases aren't uncommon in Crypto but the speed that they happend, and the similar logarithmic gains (4X) are uncommon. Also the lack of any natural growth is concerning, as coin price stability usually relies on slow increases to market cap and price to solidify investments. But this alone isn't nearly enough to make a solid conclusion, some coins really have gone up 400-1600% and just stayed there, so we'll compare the other charts to begin to see the full picture.

Here we see Bitcoin during this new bull market, it shows very healthy and sustainable growth (eventhough many people would like to deny that). What we can see from BTC are basically, 2X gain cycles that take around a month to complete, followed by ~30% dips, with a rally and another 2X gain cycle. Now as of recently, that trend has slowed but still we see 30% reductions with more growth and most importanly, at least until this most recent dip, the dip cycles never sank lower than the gains of the cycle. Now again, trading analysis isn't always accurate but what we can start to see is that sustained growth for the highest market cap coin (BTCs market cap is a little over 1 Trillion at the time of writing this) is brought about by periods of correction, then Dogecoin has a big issue on its hands given its recent growth and releative lack of price corrections.

Now we come to Doge during the 2017/2018 bullrun, keep in mind, the same timeframe has been selected to illustrate that the 2017/2018 bullrun was faster, and to help compare gains over a time period. For Doge we see an even less aggressive gains pattern with a much more aggressive correction. With a total of around 18X (which Doge has done well in excess of in this cycle) with a swift and violent correction down to only a 3X gain from where it started after around a month. This pattern is very common with growth this fast and this extreme and only serves as a potential red flag for Doges current price movement.

This last graph is Bitcoin during that same 2017/2018 run, which shows a similar level and speed of growth with a very similar loss of value. Now you could say that due to BTCs dominance over all of crypto that Doge's price was simply responding to Bitcoin, and there would certaily be truth in that. But it would be very silly to overlook the similarities in even more currencys to growth patterns like this, and now that BTC has had sustained growth for months now, the model of corrections and logarithmic growth has seemed to work as a positive indicator of long term value, and Doge unfortunatly, has none of these indicators at the moment.


Using this data, what I've hoped to show is that Dogecoin is looking alot like itself and BTC in 2018 and that is not a good sign, Doge is most certainly massivly overvalued and all investors in Doge should be very careful about throwing momney in bacuse as many learned in 2018 (me included) it takes a while for the market to come back, and even when it does, it takes too long.
